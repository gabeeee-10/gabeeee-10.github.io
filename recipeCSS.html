Apple Crumble Pie

By Gabriel Preis

History

The Apple Crumble Recipe’s history is rooted in British cuisine and dates back to the early 1940s. During that period of time, food was harder to come by, making it difficult for people to obtain ingredients such as flour and sugar. As a result, chefs had to come up with creative ways to make desserts using what little they had available. This led to the creation of apple crumble, a dish invented from necessity. The Origins of Apple Crumble can be traced back even further than WWII though. It is believed that this dessert was inspired by an earlier recipe called “apple slump” which originated in New England during the 19th century. However, unlike apple crumble, this recipe involved cooking apples on top of dough rather than under a layer of crumbs.

Recipe--Description, Ingredients, Steps.

What makes an apple crumble different from an apple pie? The topping! The key to a great apple crumble is the tasteful topping. An apple crumble recipe is simply a dish in which chopped apples are combined with some type of sugar, butter, or other fat, spices like cinnamon or nutmeg, and often some sort of flour mixture that forms the basis of the “crumble” topping.

Ingredients

  • 4-5, thinly sliced and skin-peeled tart apples *preferrably granny smith*.
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar.
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon.
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter.
  • 3/4 cup all-purpose flour.
  • 3/4 cup old-fashioned rolled oats.
  • 1/2 cup packed light brown sugar.
  • 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt.
  • Additional butter for topping.

Directions

Apple Crumble Topping
